CS major is good for international students

Whether you're one of the many international students weighing the relative merits of a handful of fields prior to making your final decision, or if you've already decided to study IT, it is worth noting some of the benefits to study computer science in the US. 


Computer Science Careers are flourishing in the US

A degree in computer science could lead to an incredibly rewarding career in the United States. Computer science careers are flourishing in the United States, both demand-wise and salary-wise. As a computer science graduate from US universities, you will be at an advantage when it comes to employment opportunities. Due to today's technological advancements, especially in the United States, careers in computer science are becoming more prominent. According to the latest 2020 estimates by the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for computer and information technology occupations currently stands at $91,250. Computer science jobs are also projected to grow 11% in a matter of ten years (up to 2029), which is one of the fastest rates among other occupations. 

What is OPT

Optional Practical Training (OPT) is temporary employment that is directly related to an F-1 student's major area of study. Eligible students can apply to receive up to 12 months of OPT employment authorization before completing their academic studies (pre-completion) and/or after completing their academic studies (post-completion). However, all periods of pre-completion OPT will be deducted from the available period of post-completion OPT. 

Computer Science is a STEM major

In general, OPTs are issued for 12 months, which can be extended by another 24 months, for a total of 36 months for eligible applicants. 

Computer Science Career Prospects in United States

Career prospects for computer science majors in the United States are extensive. Skills learned at computer science programs in the US can span across several different industries, highly increasing chances of employment. Computer science graduates can choose from numerous career paths, including data science, network administration, network engineering, software engineering, software development, web development, etc. Regardless of your career path, the US is excellent in the sense that it provides countless chances to advance professionally. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, a person working as a computer and information research scientist earns a median annual salary of $126,830 per year or $60.97 per hour. The salary will depend on the career path you intend to pursue. For example, software developers earn a median annual salary of over $110,000, while network and computer systems administrators earn a median salary of $84,810 per year. Salaries tend to increase with professional experience.
